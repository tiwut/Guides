New Year's Eve: Germany's 'Bonfire Night'

1. What is Silvester?

In Germany, the special day celebrated on December 31st is called 'Silvester'. Much like the British Guy Fawkes Day, it is a night defined by light, fire, and loud celebrations to mark the end of the old year.]

2. Fireworks and Lights

At the stroke of midnight, thousands of fireworks are set off in gardens and streets all over the country. This tradition is very similar to the 'Fireworks Night' in Britain. The sky turns into a colorful display of light.

3. Food and Customs

While children in Britain used to collect money for the 'Guy', German children often enjoy special treats like 'Berliner' (jam-filled donuts). A unique tradition is watching the British comedy sketch 'Dinner for One' on television, which is a must-see for almost every German family.

4. Safety and Responsibility

Just like on Bonfire Night, the fire brigade is very busy. Fireworks contain gunpowder and can be dangerous. It is important that adults supervise the celebrations and that everyone acts responsibly to prevent accidents.
