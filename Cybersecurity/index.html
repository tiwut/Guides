Cybersecurity is the practice of defending computers, servers, mobile devices, electronic systems, networks, and data from malicious attacks. It is also known as information technology security or electronic information security.

In an increasingly digital world, the scope of cybersecurity extends from personal identity protection to national security. The goal is to ensure the integrity of data and the continuity of operations against threats like Ransomware, Phishing, and Zero-Day Exploits.

The CIA Triad & Key Concepts

The industry standard model for information security is known as the CIA Triad:

  • Confidentiality: Ensuring that sensitive information is accessed only by an authorized person.
  • Integrity: Maintaining the accuracy and completeness of data. preventing unauthorized modification.
  • Availability: Ensuring that information and resources are available to those who need them when they need them.
Recall: Security is not a product, but a process. It requires constant vigilance and adaptation to new threats.

Tools & Setup

To analyze network security and system integrity, professionals use specific command-line tools. Below are common examples used for reconnaissance and system hardening:


# Update local repositories and upgrade system
$ sudo apt update && sudo apt upgrade -y

# Check for open ports (Network Mapping)
$ nmap -sC -sV localhost

# Check active listening ports on Linux
$ sudo netstat -tulpn

Use man [command] to read the manual pages for these tools before executing them.

Defense Strategies

Implementing a robust security posture requires following industry standards and hygiene:

Caution: Social Engineering is the #1 cause of breaches. Technology cannot fix human error; training is essential.
  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Enforce MFA on all critical accounts to prevent unauthorized access even if passwords are compromised.
  • Principle of Least Privilege: Users should only have access to the specific data and resources needed to perform their job tasks.
  • Regular Backups: Follow the 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies of data, 2 different media, 1 offsite.
  • Patch Management: Keep operating systems and applications updated to close known vulnerabilities.
